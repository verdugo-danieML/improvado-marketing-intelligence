## ðŸ“Š Executive Summary Dashboard

The **Executive Summary** is your command center for high-level marketing metrics. It provides a real-time overview of your campaign performance across all channels.

### 1. KPI Cards (Top Section)
The top section displays 8 critical Key Performance Indicators (KPIs).
*   **What it shows:** Current value, unit (e.g., $), and percentage change vs. the previous period.
*   **Sparklines:** Small graphs showing the trend over the last 12 periods.
*   **Metrics:** Spend, CPM, CTR, CPC, Video Views, Impressions, Conversions, Conversion Rate.
*   **Usage:** Quickly assess the health of your marketing efforts. Green arrows indicate positive performance; red arrows indicate areas needing attention.

### 2. Performance Over Time (Center Chart)
An interactive time-series chart visualizing trends.
*   **Interaction:** Use the buttons in the right sidebar (Channel, Data Source, Campaign) to change the data displayed in this main chart.
*   **Usage:** Identify seasonal trends, spikes, or drops in performance over specific dates.

### 3. Detailed Performance Tables (Right Sidebar)
Three tables provide granular data:
*   **Channel Performance:** Breakdown by channel (e.g., Paid Social, Programmatic). Shows Impressions, Spend %, and CTR.
*   **Data Source Performance:** Metrics by platform (e.g., Facebook, Google Ads).
*   **Campaign Performance:** Top-level campaign metrics.
*   **Usage:** Drill down to see which specific channels or campaigns are driving your top-level KPIs.

## ðŸ¤– AI Customer Voice Dashboard

The **AI Customer Voice** dashboard leverages machine learning to analyze customer sentiment from YouTube comments.

### 1. Filters (Top Section)
*   **Select Brand:** Filter all data (charts and comments) by a specific brand mentioned in the video data.
*   **Filter Comments by Sentiment:** Narrow down the "Recent Comments" list to show only Positive, Negative, or Neutral comments.

### 2. Sentiment Distribution (Left Chart)
A donut chart showing the overall proportion of Positive vs. Negative vs. Neutral sentiment.
*   **Usage:** Get a quick "pulse check" on how your audience feels about your brand or content.

### 3. Recent Comments (Right Section)
A scrollable list of actual YouTube comments, enriched with AI analysis.
*   **Details:** Author, Video Title, Sentiment Label (color-coded), Comment Text, Likes, and Replies.
*   **Usage:** Read actual customer feedback. Use the filters to focus on "Negative" comments to address complaints or "Positive" comments to identify what's working.

### 4. Critical Alerts
*   **What it is:** The system automatically flags comments with high negative sentiment scores.
*   **Usage:** Immediate attention required! These are potential PR risks or urgent customer service issues.
